NETFLIX

Share what you love, with who you love.

CLIENT

Netflix is movie and TV show streaming company.

OPPORTUNITY

This was a design exercise. My team’s objective was to design new features to increase social actives and sharing within the Netflix platform.

SOLUTION

After research and ideation, I created 3 related features which were demonstrated though a prototype that allow users to contribute to the value and validation of films through social media and sharing.

PROJECT DETAILS

My Role: I was the lead designer on a UX team of 3. While we equally participated on research, user interviews, usability testing, ideation, sketching, and prototyping, I was also responsible for keeping the team in line with our project plan design direction.

Tools: Omnigraffle, Sketch, Invision, Post-It Notes, Pen & Paper

Methods: Competitive analysis, user research, quantitative survey, persona creation, user stories & journeys, brainstorming, user flows, scenarios, wireframes, mockups, prototypes, user testing.

Timeframe: Two weeks

RESEARCH

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

To get started, we compared video streaming features of both Netflix and Amazon to gain an understanding of where Netflix was falling short compared to it’s main competitor. We quickly found that while Netflix is easy to use, the social aspects are very limited and difficult to find. We also realized that annually, Netflix and Amazon were similarly prices, yet Amazon has many more features and social benefits.

USER SURVEY

Next, we used Survey Monkey to create a survey to learn more about people’s movie and TV watching habits. This allowed us to determine the demographics of who watches Netflix and how they learn about what to watch and how they share what they have watched.

SURVEY TAKEAWAYS

  • 70% of participants spend more than 5 hours watching streamed media a week
  • 74% use Netflix
  • 90% of participants rely on word of mouth for recommendations on what to watch

FIELD INTERVIEWS

While we waited for the survey results to come in, my teammates and I hit the streets of downtown Chicago to gain qualitative data on Netflix users. In the course of one day, we had a conversation with 17 Netflix users. We asked about how each person uses Netflix, how they find what they want to watch or how they hear about movies and TV shows, and how they share a movie or TV that they love.

INTERVIEW TAKEAWAYS

The overarching theme that appeared in the interviews was that while everyone had positive things to say, they all relied on exterior resources for discovering what to watch and for sharing their personal favorites. We quickly learned that while Netflix as reliable for having great content, it was difficult to find a movie or show by using Netflix alone.

“I always check out IMDB or google Top 100 on Netflix.”

AFFINITY MAPPING

Once we felt there was a solid pattern appearing during the interviews, we went back to our workspace and pulled out of the key details and mapped out user behaviors, needs, and pain points. This exercise allowed us to define our problem statement, discover our design direction, and create our personas.

From the research, we discovered that the problem is that Netflix does not accurately recommend movies and TV shows that portray users’ interests and therefore users base their media streaming on word of mouth.

In order to address this problem, the solution must allow users to contribute to the value and validation of media through social activity and sharing.

PERSONAS

Using the information we gathered from affinity mapping, my team created three personas to ensure that the features we created would benefit the main types of Netflix users. Our primary persona, Jenni, represented the majority of users. Our secondary personas, Doug and Lou made up for more niche users.

ITERATION & DESIGN

IDEATION GROUP SESSIONS

It was now time to start collecting ideas for our solution. My team held two separate small group ideation sessions. The first was a group of nine the second was a group of five. We broke the sessions down into three rounds, so we could discover solutions that would fit for each persona type.

The ideation sessions were wildly successful. We conducted another round of affinity mapping so that we could group similar ideas for possible solutions. We quickly realized that our new features needed to include the following: ability to follow friends, easy way to share content, and improved curation.

LO-FI SKETCHING

We each sketched out our own interpretations of the new features, then compared our work. Our concepts were very similar and in the end we were able to combine our ideas into one.

MID-FI WIREFRAMES

We then moved our sketches into wireframes using Omnigraffle and Sketch, then Invision for prototyping.

USABILITY TESTING

Before moving our Omnigraffle low-fidelity wireframes into high-fidelity mockups, we conducted a round of usability test to see how our new features accommodated users. We then made a few adjustments upon the feedback.

WIREFRAMES & MOCKUPS

We each sketched out our own interpretations of the new features, then compared our work. Our concepts were very similar and in the end we were able to combine our ideas into one.

PROPOSAL & FEEDBACK

After two short weeks of research, design, and iterating, it was finally time to present our new features.

Using Powerpoint, we built a presentation to explain our design problem, research, and prototype to a room of 30 User Experience Designers and Developers. Overall, we received incredible feedback and a true interest in the features that we proposed.
