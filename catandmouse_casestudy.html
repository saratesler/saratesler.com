CAT AND MOUSE GAMES

Where everybody knows your name.

CLIENT

Cat and Mouse Games specializes in all types of games, from children to adults. The store has a very crafty feel, made up mainly of wood and warm colors such as orange. At first glance it seems very child focused, but when you walk inside the store you will quickly realizethe employee was a diehard gamer, there to support any level of gaming needs.

OPPORTUNITY

My goal for this mock project was to design a clickable prototype for their ecommerce site that would meet the goals of the users, the business goals, and the goals of the brand by encouraging the strong customer relationships that the store does.

SOLUTION

The answer to their ecommerce puzzle was to create a clean and easy to navigate homepage, promote and encourage customer reviews, and to provide a quick 3-step checkout process.

PROJECT DETAILS

My Role: I was the sole designer for this project. I conducted research, user interviews, usability testing, ideation, sketching, and prototyping.

Tools: Omnigraffle, Invision, Post-It Notes, Pen & Paper

Methods: Contextual Inquiry, competitive analysis, user research, user journeys, user flows, wireframes, prototypes, user testing.

Timeframe: Two weeks

RESEARCH

CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

I took the train to the Bucktown to get a sense for the local store. When I walked in, I saw there was a large wooden table with 5-6 people from all walks of life (someone in a suit, another guy wearing a Bulls jersey) playing a game. One of the guys immediately popped out of his chair to welcome me and let me know if I needed any help just to ask. As I looked around, I could immediately sense that the ecommerce site did resemble the actual store. Both were filled with warm and inviting colors and very cluttered.

The dilemma, though, is that while the actual store may have been filled to the brim and busy, it was still extremely inviting. I was almost tempted to sit down with the gentlemen and hang out. It was quickly clear that the ecommerce site does not do the actual store justice.

After visiting the quaint store, I returned home to review the Cat and Mouse website with a renewed understanding of the business and began working on a problem statement for this project.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

The ecommerce site does not encourage the strong customer relationships that the store does.

Now that I knew the meat of the problem, I was able to quickly create a design direction for my prototype.

DESIGN DIRECTION

The website will develop customer relationships through generating loyalty, trust, and personal service.

USER FLOW

Based on my research, I next determined how the three personas would navigate through the site.

DESIGN

SITE MAP

After I felt comfortable with how the personas would use the site, I focused on creating an intuitive navigation system.

WIREFRAMES

I personally love a good rapid prototyping session before choosing a layout. I filled up a few pages with as many different versions of what I thought could be an improvement to the homepage, category page, product page, and check process. Once I felt good about one of each, I drafted a low-fidelity version with Omnigraffle and Invision.

USABILITY TESTING

After creating a clickable prototype, I was ready to put it out there in the world... and but that I mean I was ready to recruit user testers and see how my concepts worked. I was able to recruit 7 testers, for twenty minutes each.

Before testing the prototype, I let the tester know how much I appreciate their time and that I am testing the product, not them. Then, I ask a few demographic questions before diving into the prototype. This allows the participant to relax and allows me to measure any demographic patterns.

Next, I walk them through three preplanned scenarios and see how each participant thinks and how they use the site. By the fourth participant, there were some very prominent patterns.

USABILITY TAKEAWAYS

  • 85% of the participants said that they prefered the large images and clutter free home page. They were immediately attracted to the best sellers and could quickly digest the simple navigation. This homepage felt familiar to them so they were comfortable during the shopping experience even though they had never before heard of Cat and Mouse Games.
  • Being mindful of a clean interface, I did not display the ratings on the category landing page. The majority of test participants were disgruntled by this, so I knew I would need to add the ratings back in.

NEXT STEPS
